Gene Wolfe?

If you're interested in science fiction, the best pitch for Gene Wolfe is that your favorite author probably loves him.
While he never achieved the level of household success of Asimov or LeGuin (or perhaps his revival hasn't yet come?), he was an indispensible presence in the late 20th century literary sci-fi scene.
And, if sci-fi isn't your thing, he was also a P&G company man who was responsible for the Pringles machine (and potentially the inspiration for the brand's logo).
Again, here are some works you may find interesting:

  • "Forlesen" (1974) - A story about the surreal and institutional nature of a beloved Cincinnati business
  • The Fifth Head of Cerberus (1982) - A puzzle box in three parts.
  • Soldier of the Mist (1986) - One of the best historical (+myth) novels I have read, written as the diary of an amnesiac in Classical Greece.
  • "December Twenty-Fourth Nineteen Fifty-Five" (1988) - A poem written for his wife about the day specified.

For the sake of this assignment, here is a link to his Wikipedia page. And an article about him in Medium.
